California Proposition 22 Disclosure

Important information for drivers about your rights and protections under California law

📋 Summary

As a driver with Paradise Delivery, you are an independent contractor (1099), not an employee. However, California Proposition 22 guarantees you important earnings and benefit protections. Paradise Delivery exceeds all minimum requirements.

What is Proposition 22?

Proposition 22 is a California law that ensures drivers for app-based delivery services receive:

  • Minimum earnings guarantee (120% of minimum wage for engaged time)
  • Mileage reimbursement for vehicle expenses
  • Healthcare subsidies for qualifying drivers
  • Occupational accident insurance
  • Protection against discrimination and harassment

While drivers remain independent contractors, these protections ensure fair compensation for your work.

💰 Your Earnings Guarantee

Minimum Pay Requirements

Prop 22 requires that drivers earn at least:

  • 120% of minimum wage for engaged time (time from accepting an order to completing delivery)
  • $0.36 per engaged mile for vehicle expenses (as of 2025)

Paradise Delivery Pays More

We exceed the minimum requirements:

  • $5.00 base pay per delivery (exceeds hourly minimum for typical delivery)
  • $0.60 per mile (67% above the $0.36 minimum)

Example Calculation

Prop 22 Minimum (25 min delivery, 5 miles):
Time: 0.42 hrs × $16.50/hr × 120% = $8.32
Mileage: 5 miles × $0.36 = $1.80
Minimum Required: $10.12

Paradise Delivery Pays:
Base: $5.00
Mileage: 5 miles × $0.60 = $3.00
Your Pay: $8.00

Note: Short deliveries may not meet the hourly minimum on time alone. We supplement with our $5 base guarantee.

Weekly True-Up

If your earnings for a week fall below the Prop 22 minimum guarantee, we will automatically add a "true-up" payment to your account to bring you to the required minimum. However, our rates are designed so this rarely happens.

🏥 Healthcare Subsidy

Qualification

You may qualify for a healthcare subsidy if you:

  • Average 15+ hours of engaged time per week
  • Purchase a qualified health plan

Subsidy Amount (2025)

  • 15-25 hours/week: 41% of the average Covered California premium (~$82-136/quarter)
  • 25+ hours/week: 82% of the average Covered California premium (~$164-204/quarter)

To claim your subsidy, submit proof of health insurance enrollment through your driver portal.

🛡️ Insurance Coverage

Occupational Accident Insurance

Paradise Delivery provides occupational accident insurance that covers:

  • Medical expenses for injuries while on an active delivery
  • Disability payments for lost income due to injury
  • Death benefits for your beneficiaries

This coverage is provided at no cost to you and is in addition to your own auto insurance.

Your Auto Insurance Responsibilities

You are required to maintain your own auto insurance. We recommend:

  • Personal auto policy with rideshare/delivery endorsement, OR
  • Commercial auto policy that covers delivery activities

Contact your insurance provider to ensure you have appropriate coverage for delivery work.

🚫 Anti-Discrimination Protection

Prop 22 prohibits Paradise Delivery from discriminating against drivers based on:

  • Race, color, national origin, or ancestry
  • Religion or creed
  • Physical or mental disability
  • Sex, gender, gender identity, or sexual orientation
  • Age
  • Marital status
  • Military or veteran status

If you believe you have experienced discrimination, please contact us immediately.

📊 Your Rights as an Independent Contractor

As an independent contractor (1099), you have the right to:

  • Work when you want - no schedule requirements
  • Work for other delivery services simultaneously
  • Decline any delivery request without penalty
  • Use your own methods to complete deliveries
  • Provide your own equipment (vehicle, phone, etc.)

You are responsible for:

  • Paying your own taxes (we provide 1099 forms)
  • Obtaining and maintaining your own insurance
  • Complying with all traffic and safety laws
  • Maintaining your vehicle in safe operating condition
