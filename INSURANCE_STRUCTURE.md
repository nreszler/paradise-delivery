# City Delivery LLC / Paradise Delivery - Insurance Structure

## Follows DoorDash Model Exactly

### Legal Entity
**City Delivery LLC** (d/b/a Paradise Delivery in Paradise, CA)

### What City Delivery Covers

**1. Excess Liability Insurance: $1 Million**
- Coverage: Bodily injury & property damage to third parties
- When: Only during active delivery (order accepted → delivered)
- Type: Excess (kicks in after driver's insurance)
- Does NOT cover: Driver's vehicle, driver's injuries

**2. Occupational Accident Insurance**
- Coverage: Driver injuries on the job
- When: Active delivery period
- Typical coverage: $1M medical, disability benefits
- Cost: ~$0.05-0.10 per delivery

**3. General Liability (Platform)**
- Coverage: Customer injuries, food poisoning claims, etc.
- When: Always (platform operations)
- Amount: $1-2 million
- Annual cost: $2,000-5,000

---

### What Driver Must Have (Required)

**1. Personal Auto Insurance (State Minimum)**
- Required to drive legally
- Paradise Delivery verifies at onboarding
- Upload insurance card (AI checks expiration)

**2. Rideshare/Commercial Endorsement (Strongly Recommended)**
- Most personal policies EXCLUDE commercial use
- Driver needs "rideshare add-on" OR commercial policy
- Paradise Delivery educates drivers but doesn't require proof
- Most food delivery drivers DON'T have this (risk they take)

**3. Driver's Coverage Periods**

| Period | Coverage |
|--------|----------|
| **App OFF** | Driver's personal insurance only |
| **App ON, No Order** | Driver's personal insurance |
| **Order Accepted → Delivered** | Driver's primary + Paradise excess |

---

### Driver Verification

**Required Documents:**
1. ✅ Driver's license (front + back)
2. ✅ Auto insurance card (current, not expired)
3. ✅ Vehicle registration (current)

**NOT Required:**
- ❌ Proof of rideshare coverage (can't enforce easily)
- ❌ Commercial insurance (too expensive for drivers)

**Recommendation:**
- Check "I have appropriate insurance coverage" checkbox
- Provide insurance education during onboarding
- Recommend rideshare add-on (State Farm, Geico, Progressive offer)

### Risk Mitigation

**The Problem:**
- Driver has accident during delivery
- Personal insurance denies claim (commercial exclusion)
- Paradise excess coverage kicks in BUT:
  - Only covers other party (not driver's car)
  - Driver pays deductible ($500-1,000)
  - Driver's car not repaired

**Your Protection:**
1. **Verify insurance** at onboarding
2. **Educate drivers** about coverage gaps
3. **Track incidents** - flag high-risk drivers
4. **Require photos** at delivery (proves driver was working)
5. **Occupational accident** - covers driver injuries

**Driver's Risk:**
- They accept this risk when signing up
- Most gig drivers drive without proper coverage
- Not your legal responsibility (1099 contractors)

---

### Legal Structure

**Why This Works:**
- Drivers are **independent contractors** (1099)
- Not employees - no workers comp required
- Driver's insurance is primary responsibility
- Paradise provides excess coverage (industry standard)

**Liability Waiver:**
```
I understand that:
1. I must maintain personal auto insurance
2. Paradise Delivery provides excess liability only
3. My vehicle is not covered by Paradise insurance
4. I am responsible for any damage to my vehicle
5. I should consider rideshare insurance add-on
```

Driver signs during onboarding.

---

### Claims Process

**If Accident Occurs:**

1. **Driver reports** via app or phone immediately
2. **Gather info:** Photos, police report, other party details
3. **Driver files** with their insurance first
4. **If denied** or insufficient, Paradise excess kicks in
5. **Paradise insurance** handles third-party claims
6. **Driver handles** their own vehicle damage

**Timeline:**
- Report within 24 hours
- Investigation: 1-2 weeks
- Resolution: 30-60 days

---

### Comparison to Employees

**If Drivers Were Employees (W-2):**
- You'd pay workers comp insurance
- You'd provide commercial auto coverage
- Cost: +$2-4 per delivery
- That's why gig economy uses 1099

**1099 Model (Current):**
- Drivers responsible for their own insurance
- Paradise provides excess only
- Legal and standard industry practice
